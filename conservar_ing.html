Why must we conserve them?

All living organisms on earth are related because we originated from each other. We are all built with the same molecules: proteins, sugars, fats, nucleic acids (DNA, RNA). And we depend on each other to survive and grow. When we eat we are acquiring the materials that allow us to survive  and grow.



As well as relying on them for our food, we use plants and animals in many other ways, in building houses and furniture (timber from trees), in medicines (plant compounds), in the production of energy (firewood and charcoal from trees), and in decoration of places (flowers, palms).


As intelligent living beings, we are responsible for the care of the flora and fauna of our country and our planet.

National Commission for Knowledge and Use of Biodiversity
